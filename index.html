Hi There!

I’m Kristina Smith! I’m from Normal, Illinois, and I’m a junior majoring in Public Policy, minoring in Education, and getting a certificate in Human Rights. I currently serve as the VP of Services and Sustainability in DSG, where I have had the opportunity to engage with the many issues that affect us everyday. I want to be your DSG president because I know that Duke, on its own, will not provide everything that we as an undergraduate body need. Not without a push from student leaders.

As your DSG President, I plan to prioritize affordability and accessibility on this campus, because I care about the unnecessary barriers that some students face. I want to broaden the reach that Duke students have not just in the world, but in our local community. And I want to challenge the unhealthy parts of our campus culture.

I know I can achieve these goals because I have a body of project work that was inspired by making rarely voiced, rarely heard opinions, visible. During my tenure, I created Daily Devil Deals, a program that installs $5 meals at every campus vendor in order to make eating more affordable for all students and staff. I collaborated with the Duke Disability Alliance to ban the Languages building as a meeting space for student groups because it is the only non-wheelchair accessible building on our campus. I also reached out to Durham vendors to bring a Farmer’s Market to campus and bridge the divide between Duke and the city in a tangible way. And it was student voices that inspired each of these projects.

I care deeply about all of these issues. As your president, I will listen to you, I will learn from you, and I will instigate lasting, positive, and concrete change for you… and that starts here with my campaign! I have collaborated with student leaders across campus to come up with my platform and I want to hear your thoughts.

Please read through my policy proposals and feel free to reach out with questions and suggestions. I hope to meet you in the coming weeks to hear your vision for how we can build a better Duke!

Love,

Kristina Smith

Increase Course Cost Transparency

Increase Course Cost Transparency

Low-income students who must budget wisely to maintain their financial health are often caught off guard by unanticipated course expenses, such as special-edition textbooks, online labs, clickers, and lab supplies. I will work with the Deans of Trinity and Pratt and the Office of the Registrar to have all anticipate course costs listed for every class in DukeHub before bookbagging begins. If students can anticipate what their course expenses will be, they will be better able to balance and plan their finances each semester.

-Kristina

Improve Support for Undocumented Students

Improve Support for Undocumented Students

Undocumented students face constant uncertainties about their safety and the stability of their status in the U.S. The University has offered several statements of commitment to protecting undocumented students, but Duke lacks a formal system for advising and supporting them. I will work with the Provost and the Office of Institutional Equity to ensure that Undocu Training for faculty and staff, such as the program offered by the University of Washington, is administered for all faculty members and for staff in student support roles. CAPS counselors should be a priority audience for these trainings, and they should be equipped with the knowledge and tools to connect undocumented students with University resources available to protect and support them.

-Kristina

Collaborate with Students Pursuing Accessibility Reforms

Collaborate with Students Pursuing Accessibility Reforms

Student groups such as the Duke Disability Alliance and NAMI have a powerful, passionate voice that influences Duke’s physical and institutional accessibility. To enhance DSG’s commitment to these initiatives, I will create a Cabinet position, Director of Accessibility Initiatives, that liaises directly with DDA, NAMI, other relevant student groups, and Duke’s Student Disability Access Office. I will work with this Director to ensure that the voices of students with disabilities are included in DSG’s project work and administrative advocacy.

-Kristina

Formalize Academic and Career Advising Options for Underserved Students

Formalize Academic and Career Advising Options for Underserved Students

Many students at Duke do not want to pursue a medical or law degree or enter the consulting, finance, or technology sectors immediately after college. Those wishing to pursue alternatives deserve a formal academic and career advising network comparable to that available to pre-professional students. Additionally, first-generations students, regardless of their academic and career paths, need advising that acknowledges and incorporates solutions to their lack of familial experience and support for pursuing a rigorous higher education and entering competitive career fields. I will work with the Career Center and Academic Advising Center to develop initiatives, similar to the Sanford School’s Embark program, that offer specialized networking and strategizing opportunities to students pursuing less-popular trajectories and to first-generation students.

-Kristina

Build Ties with North Carolina Central University to Improve Our Standing in Durham

Build Ties with North Carolina Central University to Improve Our Standing in Durham

Duke will soon have a new Vice President for Durham and Regional Affairs, and I will work closely with Dr. Wynn and his successor to concretely improve Duke undergraduates’ understanding of, respect for, and role in Durham. I believe that partnering with the student government at North Carolina Central University on service initiatives and event planning would be a significant step in the right direction. I will also work with senators to continue efforts to help Duke students engage with Durham through an on-campus farmers’ market and opportunities to learn from and work with Durham activists, artists, and entrepreneurs.

-Kristina

Provide Consistent, Sustainable Options for To-Go Options at Duke Dining Venues

Provide Consistent, Sustainable Options for To-Go Options at Duke Dining Venues

Currently, different Duke Dining venues offer different to-go options, all of which are single-use and many of which are not environmentally friendly. I will work with Duke Dining, the Student Dining Advisory Committee, and the many sustainability-focused student groups on campus to advocate for a single vendor that offers low-cost, highly-sustainable to-go products for all Duke Dining vendors. I will also expand the reusable to-go container initiative currently being piloted so that we can reduce overall waste from to-go products.

-Kristina

Expand Gender Violence Prevention Education

Expand Gender Violence Prevention Education

The Women’s Center offers a new, comprehensive gender violence prevention curriculum that explores the interplay between social, racial, and gender dynamics in creating situations conducive to sexual assault and gender violence. I will work with the Women’s Center to ensure that all students, not just those in Greek organizations and SLGs, are exposed to this program and other similar initiatives.

-Kristina

Create Spaces for Non-Exclusive Community Building

Create Spaces for Non-Exclusive Community Building

The University is reviewing the housing model, and changes to it may be in the near future. However, regardless of how the housing model evolves, we face a critical gap in the opportunities to build community outside of and between our residential spaces. I will partner with DUU and House Councils to take advantage of physical, communal spaces, such as the quads on West Campus and new Student Affairs spaces in West Union and the Rubenstein Arts Center to plan free, open events (with lots of food, of course) that will provide opportunities for students of all class years and affiliations to build community.

-Kristina

Daily Devil Deals

Daily Devil Deals

Daily Devil Deals

Financial aid opened the door for me to come to Duke, but I and many of my peers have struggled to stay afloat throughout my time here. A common example of the uncalculated cost of college is food insecurity— eating on Duke’s campus is unaffordable for way too many students. I took action and worked with Duke Dining to make sure that every single vendor on campus has a full lunch option that is $5. This new program has made meal-time less stressful for many students.

-Kristina Smith

Lowered FLEX Increments

Lowered FLEX Increments

Lowered FLEX Increments

Previously students could only add FLEX points to their accounts in increments of $25. This was a major burden for many students- some even said that they couldn’t do laundry without loading $25 to their accounts. I worked with OIT and the DukeCard office to transform this and now students can reload accounts in increments of $10, $15, and $20.

-Kristina
